Cash Flow

My Journey in Creating a Financial Management Software

I'm a 16-year-old programmer currently attending classes at SENAI Alfried Krupp, where I'm pursuing a technical course in administration. Recently, I had a class with Professor Anderson, where he taught us about using Excel for cash flow management. This experience sparked a brilliant idea in my mind.

From that point, the idea of creating financial management software, focusing on cash flow control, using web technologies like HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, emerged. However, this project stood out as the most challenging one I've taken on so far. The reason? Mainly due to the heavy use of JavaScript, and also because I was working with a relatively new library for me, called "chart.js."

To make the project a reality, I had to dive into the "chart.js" library to understand how to apply it effectively. It was a period of intense learning but extremely rewarding.

While I was developing the software, I created a message that I decided to include in it: "Money is like a hammer; it can be used to build or destroy, depending on how we wield it." This quote reflects my belief that financial management is a powerful tool, and how we use it can impact both the construction and destruction of our goals.

After a few days of intense coding, I finally completed the software. I proudly presented it to friends and teachers, and I was thrilled to receive positive feedback. But the most rewarding moment was when my father, who is an entrepreneur in the construction industry, showed a great interest in my work and started using the software to manage his company's finances. This left me very pleased, as I saw that my project not only met expectations but also had a practical impact in the real world.

This process of creating financial management software taught me not only technical skills but also valuable lessons about perseverance, continuous learning, and the importance of applying knowledge practically. I look forward to continuing to explore the world of programming and further developing my skills, aiming to create innovative and useful solutions for people and businesses.
